Instant Gratification

Apps, social media, and other services offer instant gratification through notifications, likes, comments, and immediate replies. The desire for likes and attention from others can lead to addictive behavior. At the same time, social media increases the risk of anxiety and depression.

FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) Syndrome

The fear of missing out on important events, news, or experiences can drive people to stay constantly connected through their mobile phones.

Activate "Airplane Mode" or "Do Not Disturb"

A great way to disconnect even if you have your phone with you is to set it to "Airplane Mode." This way, you won't receive any calls or messages. Another option is to put your phone in "Do Not Disturb" mode. With this setting, you won't receive any notifications or calls, but if you take your phone out to check it, everything that has arrived will appear.

The cell phone, the further away the better

If you're someone who needs to check your phone every two seconds even if you haven't received any notifications, the best thing you can do is not carry it with you. Tip: leave it at home, in your car, in your desk drawer, give it to someone for safekeeping, and more. Of course, it will take a lot of willpower to do this.

Delete useless applications

It's quite normal for your phone to become filled with useless apps that just waste your time. So now you have to pick up your phone and delete them. Just keep only those you actually use and that serve a purpose.
